Top 10 Places to Visit in Baku

Baku, the capital of Azerbaijan, is a fascinating blend of ancient history and modern architecture. Located on the shores of the Caspian Sea, the city offers unique cultural experiences for every traveler.

Start your journey in the Old City (Icherisheher), a UNESCO World Heritage Site filled with narrow streets, historic mosques, and the iconic Maiden Tower. Walking here feels like stepping back in time.

Next, visit the Flame Towers — Baku’s modern symbol. These skyscrapers light up the skyline at night and offer stunning views of the city.

Other must-see spots include the Baku Boulevard, Highland Park, Heydar Aliyev Center, Little Venice, and the vibrant Nizami Street.

Baku is a city where tradition meets innovation, making it a perfect destination for culture lovers, photographers, and adventure seekers.

Food & Culture · Feb 5, 2026

A Complete Guide to Azerbaijani Cuisine

Azerbaijani cuisine reflects the country’s rich history and diverse regions. It is known for its aromatic spices, fresh herbs, and slow-cooked dishes.

Plov is the national dish and comes in dozens of variations. Each region prepares it differently, often with saffron rice, dried fruits, nuts, or meat.

Dolma, qutab, kebabs, and piti soup are also essential parts of local cuisine. Meals are usually enjoyed with fresh herbs, yogurt-based sauces, and traditional bread.

No meal is complete without tea. Azerbaijani tea culture is deeply rooted in hospitality and is often served with jam rather than sugar.

Photography · Feb 1, 2026

The Best Photo Spots in Baku

Baku is one of the most photogenic cities in the Caucasus. Its mix of old stone architecture and futuristic buildings offers endless photography opportunities.

Highland Park provides panoramic views of the city and the Caspian Sea, especially beautiful at sunset. The Flame Towers look stunning when photographed at night.

The Old City is perfect for street photography, while the Heydar Aliyev Center offers clean lines and modern design for architectural shots.

Whether you are a professional photographer or just taking photos for social media, Baku will not disappoint.
