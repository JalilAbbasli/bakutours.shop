Baku Tourism

Top Cultural Landmarks and Modern Marvels in Baku

Baku, the vibrant capital of Azerbaijan, is a city where ancient history seamlessly blends with futuristic architecture. Often referred to as the "Paris of the East," Baku offers a unique experience for every traveler. Your journey should begin in Icherisheher (Old City), a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Gastronomy

Exploring Azerbaijani Cuisine: The Tradition and Taste of Dolma

Azerbaijan’s culinary heritage is a feast for the senses, and Dolma stands as its undisputed crown jewel. In 2017, Azerbaijan’s tradition of making and sharing dolma was inscribed on the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage list.

Nature & Adventure

Gabala: A Natural Paradise in the Greater Caucasus

Known as the "Switzerland of Azerbaijan," Gabala (Qabala) is the ultimate destination for those seeking fresh mountain air and breathtaking landscapes.

Archeology

Gobustan: A Journey Through Prehistoric Rock Art

Gobustan State Historical-Artistic Reserve is a world-renowned archaeological site that serves as a massive open-air museum. It is home to more than 6,000 petroglyphs.

Historic Cities

Sheki: The Architectural Jewel of the Silk Road

The historic city of Sheki is a living testament to the legacy of the Great Silk Road. Recently added to the UNESCO World Heritage list, Sheki is famous for its unique architecture.
